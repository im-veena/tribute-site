Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

About

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Widely known as the “Missile Man of India”, he played a pivotal role in strengthening India’s defense and space capabilities.

Beyond his scientific achievements, he was admired for his humility, discipline, and deep connection with students, inspiring generations through his vision and simplicity.

Learn more about his life and contributions on Wikipedia .

Early Life

Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam grew up in a modest family. From a young age, he learned the value of hard work and self-reliance by supporting his education through part-time jobs.

Career

Dr. Kalam worked as an aerospace scientist at ISRO and DRDO, where he led several critical missile development projects. His leadership in India’s missile program earned him national recognition and established India as a self-reliant technological power.

Inspiration & Values

Kalam firmly believed that dreams lead to innovation and national growth. He consistently encouraged young minds to think creatively, work with integrity, and serve the nation.

Legacy

Even after his presidency, Dr. Kalam devoted his life to teaching, writing, and motivating students. His books, speeches, and ideas continue to guide millions.

Timeline

  • 1931 – Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1969 – Joined ISRO
  • 1998 – Led Pokhran-II nuclear tests
  • 2002–2007 – President of India
  • 2015 – Passed away while delivering a lecture

His Vision

“Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action.” — Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam
